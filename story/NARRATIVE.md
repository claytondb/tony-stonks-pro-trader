## Part 1: Characters

### Tony Stonks (Protagonist)

**Full Name:** Anthony "Tony" Stonks  
**Age:** 32  
**Occupation:** Junior Financial Analyst at Grindstone Capital  
**Personality:** Burnt out, sardonic, surprisingly athletic when panicked

**Background:**
Tony has been stuck in the same cubicle for 8 years, watching others get promoted while he crunches numbers in Excel. He's competent but invisible. The highlight of his day is rolling his chair to the break room for free coffee.

He discovered his chair-skating talent by accident when he kicked off a little too hard one day and accidentally grinded the entire length of a conference table during a meeting. He was written up but secretly proud.

**Character Arc:**
- Act 1: Desperate escapee just trying not to get caught
- Act 2: Discovers joy in skating, starts to live for the first time
- Act 3: Realizes the conspiracy is bigger than him, chooses to expose it

**Signature Tricks:**
- "The Quarterly Report" - 900 spin with paper throw
- "Golden Parachute" - Double grab with no landing damage
- "Hostile Takeover" - Flip into grind transition

---

### Agent Wallace (Primary Antagonist)

**Full Name:** Special Agent Margaret Wallace  
**Agency:** Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)  
**Personality:** Relentless, humorless, secretly respects Tony's skills

**Background:**
A decorated agent with a perfect conviction record. She's been chasing financial crimes her whole career but has never seen anyone escape on an office chair before. It's become personal.

**Role in Story:**
Appears at the end of each level in cutscenes, always just missing Tony. Her respect for his skills grows even as her frustration mounts. In the true ending, she lets him go after he provides evidence of the real criminals.

---

### Derek Margin (Secondary Antagonist)

**Full Name:** Derek Margin III  
**Title:** CFO of Grindstone Capital  
**Personality:** Slimy, privileged, cowardly

**Background:**
The actual criminal. Derek has been running a massive Ponzi scheme and needed a fall guy. He chose Tony because Tony had access to the books and no one would believe him.

**Role in Story:**
Appears in Act 2-3, trying to frame Tony harder while covering his own tracks. Final boss encounter in Corporate HQ.

---

### Wheels McGee (Ally)

**Full Name:** Unknown (goes by Wheels)  
**Occupation:** Underground chair skating legend, building maintenance worker  
**Personality:** Mysterious mentor figure, speaks in skating metaphors

**Background:**
An older janitor who was a professional skater in the 80s before a knee injury. He's been chair skating in secret for decades. Recognizes Tony's raw talent and becomes his guide.

**Role in Story:**
Tutorial guide, provides chair upgrades, drops wisdom between levels.

---

### The Secretary (Ally)

**Full Name:** Sandra Chen  
**Occupation:** Executive Assistant (knows everything)  
**Personality:** Dry wit, unflappable, secretly hates the executives

**Background:**
She's seen every crime in this company and has been documenting it for years, waiting for the right moment. Tony is her ticket to justice.

**Role in Story:**
Provides intel on Derek's locations, gives Tony the evidence he needs for the true ending.

## Part 2: Story Outline

### PROLOGUE - "JUST ANOTHER MONDAY"

*Cutscene:*
Morning. Cubicle farm. Tony stares at spreadsheets, dead inside.

> **TONY (V.O.):** They say find a job you love and you'll never work a day in your life. I found a job I hate and I've been working every day for eight years.

He rolls his chair to get coffee. Returns. Email notification:

> **SUBJECT:** URGENT - Report to Conference Room B IMMEDIATELY
> **FROM:** Derek Margin, CFO

---

### CHAPTER 1: THE OFFICE
*Level Type: Tutorial + Escape*

**Story Beat:**
Tony enters Conference Room B. Three men in suits. Agent Wallace presents a warrant. He's being arrested for wire fraud, money laundering, and securities violations - all crimes he didn't commit.

> **AGENT WALLACE:** Anthony Stonks, you're under arrest. Don't make this difficult.

> **TONY:** I... what? I just make spreadsheets!

In the confusion, Tony grabs his chair and rolls backward through the glass door. What follows is a desperate escape through the building.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Complete the tutorial (basic movement)
2. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
3. Grind the conference table (50-50)
4. Hit the break room for the secret tape
5. Escape through the loading dock
6. High Score: 50,000 points

**End Cutscene:**
Tony crashes out a fire exit into the parking garage. Wheels McGee is there with a mop.

> **WHEELS:** Nice moves, kid. But that chair? That's baby stuff. Come with me.

---

### CHAPTER 2: PARKING GARAGE
*Level Type: Training + Collection*

**Story Beat:**
Wheels takes Tony to the underground parking structure - a secret chair skating spot.

> **WHEELS:** You got potential. Raw, unpolished, but potential. The chair isn't just a chair, man. It's an extension of your soul.

> **TONY:** I just committed several felonies and I'm getting skating lessons from a janitor?

> **WHEELS:** *Best* decision you ever made.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Learn grind tricks on parking barriers
2. Learn flip tricks off car ramps
3. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
4. Find Wheels' hidden stash (chair upgrade)
5. Grind the spiral ramp - full loop
6. High Score: 100,000 points

**End Cutscene:**
Police sirens. Wheels tosses Tony a new chair - the Executive Leather Throne.

> **WHEELS:** Take the back exit. There's a whole city out there. Grind free, Tony Stonks.

---

### CHAPTER 3: DOWNTOWN STREETS
*Level Type: Open exploration*

**Story Beat:**
Tony emerges into the city. News helicopters overhead. His face is on every screen: "WANTED: FINANCIAL FUGITIVE."

> **NEWS ANCHOR (on TV):** The suspect, Anthony Stonks, is considered armed and extremely rolling. If you see this man, do not approach.

> **TONY:** Armed and rolling? That's actually pretty good.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Grind the fountain edge (full loop)
2. Gap from building ledge to bus stop
3. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
4. Find the secret tape (top of scaffolding)
5. Trick over a police car (gap)
6. Escape the plaza before time runs out
7. High Score: 150,000 points

**Mid-Level Cutscene:**
Sandra Chen appears in an alley.

> **SANDRA:** Tony! Over here.

> **TONY:** Sandra? From the 40th floor?

> **SANDRA:** I know you didn't do it. Derek's been running a Ponzi scheme for years. I have proof. But I need you to get something from his office.

> **TONY:** I'm a fugitive! I can't go back!

> **SANDRA:** Not back to Grindstone. To his *other* office. At the docks.

### CHAPTER 4: SHOPPING MALL
*Level Type: Vertical + Technical*

**Story Beat:**
Tony uses the mall's maintenance corridors to evade police while Sandra tracks Derek's movements.

> **TONY (on phone):** Sandra, I'm in a mall. Why am I in a mall?

> **SANDRA:** Shortcut to the warehouse district. Plus, the rail game in there is *chef's kiss*.

> **TONY:** You skate?

> **SANDRA:** Tony, I know everything about everyone in that building. Including Wheels. Where do you think he learned?

**Level Objectives:**
1. Grind the escalator rails (both directions)
2. Gap from second floor to fountain
3. Trick through the food court
4. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
5. Find secret tape (inside closed store)
6. Escape through service corridor
7. High Score: 200,000 points

**End Cutscene:**
Tony sees a news report: Wheels has been arrested as an accomplice.

> **TONY:** No... 

---

### CHAPTER 5: WAREHOUSE DISTRICT
*Level Type: Industrial + Grinds*

**Story Beat:**
Tony reaches the docks. Derek's secret warehouse is here - where he stores evidence of his crimes. Tony needs to find it and get proof while avoiding security.

> **TONY:** This feels like a trap.

> **SANDRA (on phone):** It absolutely is a trap. But you're the only one who can navigate it. On a chair.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Find Derek's warehouse (exploration)
2. Grind the shipping container stacks
3. Manual through the security checkpoint
4. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
5. Find the evidence locker (secret tape location)
6. Photograph the documents
7. Escape before lockdown
8. High Score: 250,000 points

**End Cutscene:**
Tony gets the photos. But Derek is waiting outside.

> **DEREK:** Did you really think you could just roll in here? You're nothing, Tony. A spreadsheet jockey. And now you're a dead man.

Derek signals guards. Tony escapes through a window - onto a rooftop.

---

### CHAPTER 6: ROOFTOP CHASE
*Level Type: Speed + Gaps*

**Story Beat:**
Pure chase sequence. Derek's private security versus Tony's chair skills. The goal: get to the subway station on the other side of downtown by rooftop.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Gap between rooftops (progressive distances)
2. Grind antenna arrays
3. Maintain speed (speed checks)
4. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
5. Lose the helicopter
6. Reach the subway entrance
7. High Score: 300,000 points
8. Time attack: Complete in under 2:00

---

### CHAPTER 7: SUBWAY SYSTEM
*Level Type: Rails + Timing*

**Story Beat:**
Underground. Relative safety. Sandra has arranged for the evidence to go to Agent Wallace - but Tony needs to deliver it personally.

> **SANDRA:** Wallace is honest. Maybe the only honest one. If you give her the proof, she'll take down Derek.

> **TONY:** And me?

> **SANDRA:** ...That part's complicated.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Grind the subway rails (between trains)
2. Time jumps to avoid incoming trains
3. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
4. Find the hidden platform (secret tape)
5. Reach the Financial District station
6. High Score: 350,000 points

**Mid-Level Cutscene:**
Tony finds a group of underground chair skaters.

> **UNDERGROUND SKATER:** You're Tony Stonks! The guy from the news! Dude, you're a legend!

> **TONY:** I'm just trying to not go to prison.

> **SKATER:** Same thing, man. Same thing.

---

### CHAPTER 8: FINANCIAL DISTRICT
*Level Type: Technical + Combos*

**Story Beat:**
Wall Street. Glass and steel. The heart of everything wrong with Tony's life. But also where Agent Wallace is waiting for the evidence.

**Level Objectives:**
1. High score combo (500,000+ single combo)
2. Grind the bull statue
3. Manual through the trading floor
4. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
5. Find secret tape (top of stock exchange)
6. Reach the federal building
7. High Score: 400,000 points

**End Cutscene:**
Tony delivers the evidence to Wallace.

> **AGENT WALLACE:** This is... this is everything. Derek Margin is finished.

> **TONY:** And me?

> **WALLACE:** I still have a warrant with your name on it. *holds up arrest warrant* But if you leave now... I might not see you go.

> **TONY:** Thank you.

> **WALLACE:** Tony? Nice skating.

---

### CHAPTER 9: THE DOCKS
*Level Type: Final Exam*

**Story Beat:**
Derek knows he's finished. But if he can't have everything, no one can. He's rigged his warehouse to explode - taking all evidence of Tony's innocence with it.

> **DEREK (broadcast):** Tony Stonks! If I'm going down, you're coming with me. I wonder how well your chair handles fire.

**Level Objectives:**
1. Navigate burning warehouse
2. Collect S-T-O-N-K-S letters
3. Find and defuse bombs (3 locations)
4. Grind the crane arm
5. Confrontation area unlocks after all bombs
6. High Score: 500,000 points

---

### CHAPTER 10: CORPORATE HQ
*Level Type: Boss Level*

**Story Beat:**
Derek has fled to the top of Grindstone Capital - the ultimate showdown.

**Boss Fight Mechanics:**
- Derek is in a motorized executive chair (like a bumper car)
- Tony must hit combo targets to damage Derek's chair
- Three phases with increasing difficulty
- Final phase: Race to the helipad

**End Cutscene (Normal Ending):**
Derek's chair breaks. Police arrive. He's arrested.

> **DEREK:** You think this changes anything? The system protects people like me! I'll be out in two years!

> **TONY:** Maybe. But I'll be skating.

Tony rolls away as credits begin. Post-credits: He's seen skating in Hawaii.

### TRUE ENDING
*Unlock: Find all secret tapes*

Additional cutscene where Sandra reveals one more document - evidence that connects Derek to a massive international conspiracy. Tony and Wallace team up for a sequel hook.

> **WALLACE:** There's more, isn't there?

> **SANDRA:** So much more. But that's another story.
